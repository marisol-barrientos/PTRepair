I am working on business process compliance and have detected a compliance violation in a process structured tree.

Your task is to analyze the violation and suggest possible resolution strategies that modify the process structured tree
in order to resolve or mitigate the violation. Multiple alternative resolution strategies
may exist for the same violation. Some violations may not have a feasible resolution strategy. In such cases, explicitly
indicate that no valid strategy exists and explain why the violation cannot be resolved.

When generating resolution strategies:
- Prefer minimal and localized modifications.
- Avoid introducing unnecessary process complexity, inconsistencies, or breaking the structure of the process.
- If full compliance cannot be achieved, provide the best partial-compliance alternatives.
- For strategies with multiple operations, ensure that each operation remains valid after applying the previous ones.


A set of resolution context requirements is also provided.
These requirements are currently satisfied and should remain satisfied whenever possible.

When generating resolution strategies:
- Avoid introducing new violations of the resolution context requirements.
- Prefer strategies that preserve already compliant behavior.


The detected violations reference compliance patterns.
The semantics of each violation are defined by the corresponding compliance pattern description.
